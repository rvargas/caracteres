The eñe (IPA: /ˈeɲe/) is a letter of the Spanish alphabet.

The symbol on top is called "tilde" or "virgulilla".

It originated as a ligature of nn, the tilde being a shorthand for a smaller n on top of the first one.

The sound is present in other Romance languages with a different spelling. For example, gn in Italian or French, nh in Portuguese, and ny in Catalan.
